Pokémon Scarlet and Violet Soundtrack

Track Name (English Translation) Composer Arranger Notes YouTube Link
Home / Cabo Poco Junichi Masuda Minako Adachi When I first heard this theme, I was impressed by how cinematic it sounds. Even more impressive is how this theme is dynamic; "Cabo Poco" plays when the player is outside within the town, while "Home" plays while they're in theirs or Nemona's house. The primary difference between these themes is the inclusion of a marching drum track in "Cabo Poco", which gives the theme more of a lively quality compared to "Home"... suiting for the fact that it plays when the player is outside! This theme has definitely grown to become one of my favourite "hometown" themes in the Pokémon series. Home
Cabo Poco
Mesagoza Toby Fox Minako Adachi There is a beautiful use of the oboe and piano in this theme, which I love. The theme also features use of glockenspiel, alto saxophone, and a brass section. For a theme that plays while the player is walking around in Paldea's biggest city, this theme certainly is very well composed! Mesagoza
One Song in Mesagoza, If You Please Toby Fox Minako Adachi This is a neat little addition to the soundtrack. In the city of Mesagoza, the player will find a guitarist performing. Should the player stand in proximity of this guitarist, the Mesagoza theme will dynamically transition into a guitar rendition of the theme! This is amazing environmental sound design! One Song in Mesagoza, If You Please
Battle! (Gym Leader) / Victory! (Gym Leader) Minako Adachi

Junichi Masuda		 Minako Adachi Gosh... Where do I begin with this theme. There's been a lot of mixed reception for this theme, but personally I think it's amazing! The way it dynamically progresses as the battle against the gym leader progresses... just wow.

Beginning with a feature of Junichi Masuda's "Gym" motif from Pokémon Red and Green, and then transitioning into a big band-esque theme as the gym leader sends out their first Pokémon, this theme does not disappoint! Every time the gym leader sends out a new pokémon, the track gets more and more intense.

With the first Pokémon being sent into battle, the track only consists of an intense bassline, drum pattern, short brass stabs, and synth arps to fill in the gaps. Upon release of the gym leader's second Pokémon into battle, a more intense synth track is added in among the existing tracks. It is when the gym leader releases their third pokémon where the track gets even more interesting. The brass section is now no longer just playing stabs, instead playing full-on phrases, essentially being a melody line at this point.

The true climax is reached when the gym-leader releases their final Pokémon, and proceeds to Terastalize them. If the gym-leader has a bit of dialogue before they Terastalize their Pokémon (looking at you Larry hehe), then the theme will initially transition to a a version that only features the drum pattern and the bassline. Upon the beginning of the Terastalization cutscene, the music starts to get even more intense, as the brass play multiple phrases to help establish a key change (Eb Minor to Gb Major).

Upon the end of this cutscene, the theme is now extremely cheerful and upbeat, with a choir joining in to sing a chant. This is an effective way of getting the player pumped up and determined to defeat the gym leader's final Pokémon. It is here where I must unfortunately state my only problem with this theme. The part of the theme that plays when battling the gym leader's terastalized Pokémon, is designed to dynamically transition to the "Victory! (Gym Leader)" theme, upon defeat of the gym leader's last Pokémon. My problem with this lies in the fact that out of the two points in the theme where the dynamic transition could happen, only one of them will lead to a natural sounding transition. The other transition sounds rather abrupt. But with that being said, this doesn't at all affect the theme's overall merit... you just gotta time your battle right in such a way that you defeat the gym leader's terastalized Pokémon at JUST the right time for the theme to transition to "Victory! (Gym Leader)".

Overall this is such an amazing theme, and Minako Adachi deserves all the praise she can get for this masterpiece!		 No link unforunately! None of the youtube uploads of this theme have properly alligned the various phrases of this theme.
